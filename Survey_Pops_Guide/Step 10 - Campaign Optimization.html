Step 10 - Campaign Optimization


Now it's time to do some optimization on our campaign(s)!

Typically with the CPA Goal 2.0 bidding model, your campaign will run for a few days, and then you might get a message in your PropellerAds dashboard that volume has been cut.

This usually means your campaign doesn't convert well enough to receive more traffic. Essentially, other advertisers' campaigns for the same targeting as you are converting closer to their desired CPA Goal than you are, so they receive more traffic instead of you.

However, you can do some small optimizations to your campaign to potentially increase the overall traffic after we duplicate it.

Log into your BeMob account and view your campaign's stats:
  1. Select your campaign.
  2. Click Report.


It looks like I got lucky and my campaign is already profitable.

Volume is low, however. So I'm going to attempt to optimize some of the targeting, duplicate the campaign, and (hopefully) make more money.

But first, let me quickly explain how the CPA algorithm works:

PropellerAds wants to sell their traffic for the highest price they can. They judge this with a metric called eCPM, which is their earnings per 1,000 ad impressions.

According to PropellerAds, their algorithm (for popunder traffic) looks at the zoneID + GEO + OS when it's optimizing your campaign for you. As you gather data their algorithm will attempt to adjust your bids to get you conversions for your desired CPA.

If your campaign isn't converting as well as other advertiser's campaigns then the CPA algorithm will slowly reduce your bids... potentially to the point where you don't get any more traffic (typically this is the point where you get the red message in your account that volume has dropped).

If you want to get lots of traffic with the CPA Goal 2.0 algorithm you need a combination of a high CPA bid and a high conversion rate. This means you should eliminate the segments in your campaign that are burning money and not converting.

Segments you can optimize in PropellerAds:
  • OS
  • OS Version
  • Device
  • Browser
  • Language
  • Connection Type
  • ISP
  • Carrier (if applicable)
At the same time, you can also optimize your own funnel (i.e., offers and/or landing pages).

The way I run CPA Goal 2.0 campaigns is in versions. The first version starts wide with targeting. When the campaign's traffic starts to slow down I look through my data and see if there are any obvious optimizations I can make that'd increase my campaign's overall eCPM. I then relaunch with version 2 of the campaign and the process keeps repeating with new "versions" (i.e. v1, v2, v3, v4, etc.).

I do the optimizations very slowly over time so I'm sure not to unnecessarily restrict my volume.

What about Sources?

A typical optimization in campaigns is with the Sources/Placements. Some people that use the CPA bidding model optimize sources, but I don't. The PropellerAds algorithm optimizes these for you.

However, it's worth taking a look at them from time to time because in future campaign versions you might be able to blacklist a handful of sources that have spent multiple dollars in your initial versions of your campaign without converting.
